XpressTech Food Delivery Dashboard

XpressTech, a tech startup based in Lagos, Nigeria, began as a ride-hailing company in 2019 before diversifying into logistics and food delivery following a ban on commercial motorcycles. Capitalizing on this shift, XpressTech expanded its services by partnering with restaurants to launch its food delivery platform, Xshop. As a Supply Chain Analyst, my task was to analyze customer survey data to provide insights on food delivery preferences and behaviors, helping XpressTech enhance its services and better target its marketing strategies.
Key Insights and Strategic Recommendations

Key Insights:

  • Customer Demographics: A higher number of male customers dominate, with significant engagement from the 20-25 age group across lower income brackets, indicating targeted marketing opportunities.
  • Meal Preferences: Lunch and dinner see the highest demand, suggesting peak business hours that could benefit from optimized resource allocation.
  • Service Feedback: Mixed responses to the tracking system indicate the need for technological improvements to enhance customer satisfaction.
  • Education Level Influence: Graduates are the largest customer segment, highlighting the impact of educational background on food delivery preferences.
  • Income-Related Service Preferences: Lower-income groups predominantly use food delivery apps, emphasizing the need for affordable options.
  • Gender-Specific Ordering Patterns: Notable differences in ordering preferences between genders suggest potential for tailored marketing and menu options.

Strategic Recommendations:

  • Targeted Marketing: Launch campaigns aimed at young, price-sensitive demographics and develop strategies to attract more female customers through gender-specific promotions.
  • Service Enhancements: Upgrade delivery tracking technology and consider customer preferences for app usability improvements.
  • Operational Adjustments: Ensure efficient service during peak meal times and explore off-peak promotions to increase sales.
  • Educational Outreach: Create content and partnerships that appeal to educated customers, promoting healthier or more sophisticated meal options.
  • Pricing Strategy Adaptation: Implement tiered pricing to cater to the financial diversity within the customer base, ensuring affordability.
  • Gender-Tailored Marketing: Develop promotions and advertising campaigns that cater to the unique preferences identified in male and female customer segments.

HR Attrition Analysis

Innov8, a leading health technology company, faces challenges with employee attrition, impacting productivity and stability. This analysis from the HR Attrition Dashboard aims to uncover the underlying factors of attrition and guide the development of effective retention strategies.

Key Insights:

  • Higher attrition among males and in high-stress departments like Sales and R&D.
  • Younger employees, particularly those aged 24-35, show the highest attrition rates, indicating dissatisfaction with career progression or job roles.
  • Employees who travel frequently or have longer commutes are more likely to leave, suggesting issues with work-life balance.
  • Employees with Life Sciences and Medical backgrounds show lower attrition compared to those in Marketing and Technical degrees, suggesting better job satisfaction or alignment in health-focused roles.
  • Despite higher pay, employees living farther from work exhibit similar attrition rates, indicating that factors like commute stress may outweigh financial incentives in retention decisions.

Recommendations:

  • Implement support and career development programs in high-attrition departments to improve job satisfaction.
  • Offer flexible or remote working arrangements to alleviate the strain of frequent travel and long commutes.
  • Use regular surveys and exit interviews to monitor satisfaction and attrition trends for early intervention.

DeepCare Hospital ER Analysis Dashboard

ER Efficiency and Patient Satisfaction Analysis

DeepCare Hospitals, under the leadership of Dr. John, seeks to enhance ER efficiency and patient satisfaction by leveraging data insights to optimize emergency care operations.

Key Insights:

  • Patient Flow and Timing: Peak patient arrivals occur early mornings and evenings, with noticeable seasonal peaks in the summer months, indicating possible staffing mismatches.
  • High Load Departments: General Practice and un-referred patients (no specific department referrals) face the highest patient volume, potentially straining resources.
  • Patient Demographics and Satisfaction: A diverse patient demographic predominately consists of African American and White patients, with satisfaction scores inversely correlated with wait times, suggesting critical impact on patient experience.

Recommendations:

  • Staffing Optimization: Align staff schedules more closely with known peak times and seasons to manage patient load effectively.
  • Process Efficiency: Enhance throughput in General Practice and streamline processes for patients without specific department referrals to reduce bottlenecks.
  • Patient-Centric Initiatives: Implement fast-track options for non-critical cases and enhance cultural competence training to improve care delivery across diverse populations.
  • Performance Monitoring: Utilize continuous monitoring to adjust strategies in real-time, improving both wait times and satisfaction rates.
